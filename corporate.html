Design Elements

  • Our Visual identity fits with our vision and values because the colours we used are very calm, because we use warm nature tones. This can be linked to a safe environment. We want our users to feel comfortable enough to share their experiences. A calming colour pallet can help with this. Since the target audience enjoys camping and the outdoors, and the colour scheme matches their aesthetic, the colours also work for them.
    • #E09132
    • #454230
  • The typography aligns with our vision and values because a blog allows people to share knowledge and experiences, but without a clear font, readers won't understand what you are writing about. Furthermore, the typography has a handwritten appearance, which improves and personalises the blog reading experience. Because of this, it appeals to our target audience.
  • We want to give our community a cosy vibe with the future designs and aesthetics of our website. This has the potential to foster a sense of belonging and unity. And therefore aligns with our vision of coming together. The cosy vibe that comes with camping makes this aesthetic appealing to our target market as well.
  • UI and UX

    • When opening the website, the first thing you see is a big video and the navigation bar of course. The nav bar has a few functions on the website. When clicking on the name ‘campsite’ you always go to the home page. Because people don’t always know that, we wanted to make the UX easier to make a ‘home’ page with one of the headers as well. The pages are listed next to each other and it's clear there are 4 main pages since there is not more text. We decided to make use of text instead of icons, so it's clear in 1 word what the page is about which we cannot express in 1 icon in our case. The left side of the navigation bar has 2 functions. You have a search bar and next to that a chat function. We wanted to put this in the nav bar so the chat function is always accessible.

    On the whole website, we use a lot of videos and pictures. Our target group is 20-26, this target group is a lot on Tiktkok and Instagram. So everything has to be clear with one glance. When you hover over for example a video, it starts playing, which increases the UI. When you hover over an image or for example a blog, a slight shadow appears, so that it's clear when you click on it, it will lead you to something else.

    On top of that, throughout the website we made use of different buttons. (fe. the search button and the login button.) These buttons are in an orange box. The orange stands out from the white background.

    We want to create a safe environment for our users. When opening every page, you see a big picture at first, which creates curiosity and interest. Everything that is written has a soft tone of voice with a feeling of a friend sharing stories.

Testing Report

    Testing goals

    We hope with testing our website to find out if everything on our website all buttons work for our users and all important pages are easy to find.

    The following problems are solved with this website: making friends who also travel and have important information for our target audience on one platform. These problems have the following solutions. On the website, users have the ability to communicate with each other by commenting on each other's blog and adding each other as friends allowing them to chat with each other. Users can see the experiences of other travelers containing important information through blogs and videos. Campsite itself will also post information on the website to provide a reliable source of information.

    Finally, we want to learn whether our website is attractive to users and whether they will use it more often.

    Testing methods,

    For our website we want four people to test our website, these participants fall in the age category of our target group which is 20 to 27 years old. For testing we will use the following materials: A laptop to test the website, a phone to test and a phone to record the test. The testing will be done on campus. two participants will be testing our webpage on a laptop size and two participants will be testing our webpage on a phone size.

    For testing purposes, you will be asked to perform the following tasks:

  • Go to the Blog page and open a blog.
  • Log in to your account.
  • Search for a new route to Rome, Italy.

    • We will record the assignments. We will only give advice on the assignment if the participants do not know how to proceed.

    Testing results

    After testing our website on both laptop and phone we found the following:

  • If the user wanted to click on something, they wanted to click on the whole picture instead of only the text. This would improve the UX, because now people think it isn’t a link.
  • Also it would have been nice if all/some of the buttons worked, or that you at least got a notification or something.
  • For the users, it was a bit unclear and confusing how and where to find new blogs. Because the text in the navigation bar says diaries and not blogs, people didn’t really expect that blogs would be in there.
  • We found out that the navigation bar is not responsive to a smaller format like a phone. The search bar is overlapping the logo name. Which is confusing and weird for the users.
  • For the users, it was easy to find new routes on the locations page. It was clear for them where to go and how it works.

    Lean Canvas

    Problem

    • We are solving the lack of community and experience before and during traveling with a camper vans.

      • The interviews we did revealed that making friends while traveling is a problem and finding a friend to travel with is also a difficult issue. By traveling with a camper there are fewer opportunities to meet other travelers, often as a traveler you are then more on your own or with the company you are traveling with. There are also a limited number of social spaces where campers can stand where there are also other people camping with RVs this also makes it harder to meet people who are also traveling with an RV. What is a problem in being able to travel with friends is that people have different travel schedules. Some young people have different destinations or want to travel at a different time in their lives. This may also have to do with work. Furthermore, traveling together is also sometimes difficult because of a language barrier or cultural differences.

    • Our customers rely on reliable information
      • It became clear during the interviews that it is difficult for young people traveling by campervan to find information. The interviewees themselves indicate that because they are still young, they have less experience in RV travel and travel in general. They also indicate that they learned a lot from their parents or friends. When the RV travelers look for information online they indicate that there is a plethora of information making it very difficult for them to know what the right information is and what they can use. Furthermore, the information is not always specific enough and geared toward older campers. Finally, young campers keep up with the news poorly so they are not always aware of new regulations in another country that are important for their trip.

    Solution

    • We address the needs of the users by creating a community on our website. People can chat, share experiences, and help each other.
      • To solve both problems, we have created a platform. On this platform, young campers can share their adventures or make new friends and find more information relevant to their lifestyle. By doing this, we also hope to give our target group a chance to learn from each other and thus become a little more experienced when traveling. The website will do this by giving the opportunity for campers to share their stories through blogs or videos and follow that of others, as well as being able to comment on other blogs or videos, creating a community, helping each other. Furthermore, there will also be possibility to see in the website the trips of your friends and also meet people who make the same trips as our user. This will provide more opportunities to travel together or make friends while traveling.

    Customer segments

    • 20-27 Year olds Gap year Low maintenance Adventures Curious
      • Our target group involves young people between the ages of 20 and 27. They are young adults who love nature and travel and adventure. They are travelers who are curious about other cultures and eager to learn a lot about not only themselves, but about the world. They are often young adults who are having a gap year from college or who have worked for a few years and then want to travel. These young travelers are also people who don't need much to be happy and care a lot about luxury.

    Unique value proposition

    • Info is being kept up to date by reviews from other users Young camper van traveller-oriented communication platform Sharing of knowledge, tips, tricks, and experiences Seeing journeys other people have been on Seeing your previous journeys Marking locations users want to go to

Our brand image is also bringing RV travelers together and helping them as well. This goes along with our website, where we as a company offer one solution for both problems.
